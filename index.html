Biography

Don Norman is a leading figure in the field of Human–Computer Interaction (HCI) and User-Centered Design. He is widely known for his book The Design of Everyday Things, which explains how poor design leads to user errors.

Norman introduced key HCI concepts such as affordances, signifiers, feedback, and the Action Cycle, helping designers understand how users perceive and interact with systems.

His work emphasizes reducing the Gulf of Execution (difficulty of performing actions) and the Gulf of Evaluation (difficulty of understanding system feedback), ensuring that interfaces align with human psychology rather than forcing users to adapt to technology.
